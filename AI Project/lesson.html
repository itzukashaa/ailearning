What is Data?

Data is like a bunch of fun puzzle pieces. Some pieces tell us what things are (labels), and some pieces tell us what things look like (features). For example, if you see a picture of a cat, the picture is a feature, and calling it a "cat" is the label.

What is Preprocessing?

Preprocessing is like getting your puzzle pieces ready to use. It’s when we make sure all our pieces are clean and organized so the computer doesn’t get confused.

Types of Learning

There are two main types of learning for computers:

  • Supervised Learning: This is like when your teacher shows you how to draw a circle and then asks you to draw one. You learn by example, using both features and labels.

  • Unsupervised Learning: This is like when you have a box of crayons and paper, and you start drawing without being told what to make. The computer learns from features but without labels.
